Cookie Policy

Last Updated: November 15, 2025

This Cookie Policy explains how Trekka uses cookies and similar technologies to recognize you when you visit our platform. It explains what these technologies are and why we use them, as well as your rights to control our use of them.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners.

Cookies can be "persistent" or "session" cookies:

  • Persistent cookies: Remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them
  • Session cookies: Expire when you close your browser

2. How We Use Cookies

Trekka uses cookies for several purposes:

Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the platform to function and cannot be switched off. They include:

  • Authentication cookies to keep you logged in
  • Security cookies to protect against fraud
  • Load balancing cookies for optimal performance
  • Shopping cart/booking process cookies

Functionality Cookies

These cookies enable enhanced functionality and personalization:

  • Remember your preferences (language, currency)
  • Recall your search history and recent bookings
  • Store your location for relevant search results
  • Remember items in your wishlist

Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors use our platform:

  • Track which pages are most popular
  • Identify navigation patterns and user flows
  • Measure site performance and load times
  • Identify technical issues and errors
  • Understand demographic information about visitors

We use Google Analytics and similar tools for this purpose.

Marketing Cookies

These cookies track your online activity to help deliver relevant advertising:

  • Show you relevant ads on other websites
  • Limit the number of times you see an ad
  • Measure advertising campaign effectiveness
  • Personalize promotional content

3. Third-Party Cookies

We partner with third-party services that may set cookies on your device:

Payment Processors

Paystack and Flutterwave use cookies to process payments securely.

Social Media

Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, and LinkedIn cookies enable social sharing features.

Analytics Providers

Google Analytics helps us understand platform usage and improve our services.

Advertising Networks

Google Ads and Facebook Pixel track conversions and show relevant advertisements.

4. Other Tracking Technologies

Besides cookies, we use other similar technologies:

Web Beacons (Pixels)

Small graphic images embedded in web pages to track page views and email opens.

Local Storage

Browser storage for larger amounts of data that persists across sessions.

Session Storage

Temporary browser storage that clears when the browser is closed.

5. Your Cookie Choices

You have several options to control cookies:

Browser Settings

Most browsers allow you to:

  • View what cookies are stored and delete them individually
  • Block third-party cookies
  • Block cookies from specific websites
  • Block all cookies
  • Delete all cookies when you close your browser

Cookie Preference Tool

Use our cookie preference tool (available at the bottom of every page) to:

  • Accept or reject specific cookie categories
  • View detailed information about each cookie
  • Change your preferences at any time

Opt-Out Links

Opt out of specific tracking services:

  • Google Analytics: Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on
  • Google Ads: Ad Settings
  • Facebook: Ad Preferences

6. Impact of Disabling Cookies

If you disable cookies, some features may not work properly:

  • You may not be able to stay logged in
  • Booking process may not function correctly
  • Your preferences won't be remembered
  • Some pages may load slower or not at all
  • Personalization features will be limited

Essential cookies cannot be disabled as they are necessary for the platform to function.

7. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers have a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature that signals to websites that you don't want to be tracked. Currently, there is no industry standard for how DNT signals should be interpreted. Trekka does not respond to DNT signals at this time.

8. Mobile Devices

Mobile devices use identifiers similar to cookies:

  • iOS: Identifier for Advertisers (IDFA)
  • Android: Google Advertising ID

You can limit ad tracking or reset these identifiers in your device settings.
